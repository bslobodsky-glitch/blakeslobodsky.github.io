Example of Good Character

During COVID, I was blessed with the opportunity to help feed unfortunate families as a part of a program my parents were involved in. This memory has left a lasting impact on me forever. My mom is a doctor, and at the time she was receiving extra money for her work due to the dangers she put herself forth dealing with covid patients. My mother decided with this money instead of buying nice things for herself, she would instead give it to the less fortunate. Every two weeks, on a Saturday afternoon, I would go to a grocery store like Lidl, or shoprite and help my parents buy groceries and deliver them to the family we were given via the program. We were given a list of required things, but we also threw in a couple extras just to be nice. When we did it, I was young, and I did not understand the point, but after the 1st delivery, and seeing how much this benefited families, I realized how important it was to go out of your way to serve others and the community you are a part of.

Skills & Organizational Habits

In the classroom, I'm highly focused, consistently utilizing Cornell notetaking format whilst appropriately interacting with the class, whether it's to share an answer or guide a classmate towards it. I am rarely sidetracked, and if I do end up so, I manage to collect myself and turn my attention towards what's at hand. In English, history, and Dialogue which often hold group and larger class discussions, I try my best to share insightful information that builds upon previous points or explores new perspectives that lead to further discussion. In math and science where we are typically given work to solve ourselves, I remain focused and finish my work thoughtfully and on time. Due to my organizational habits, I am able to allocate appropriate study time at home, which helps me stay prepared for any assessments or projects planned.

Earlier in my academic career, I struggled with submitting things on time, and managing my commitments, which led to increased stress and worse performance on things such as grades and sports. With me heading into my more important years of high school, I was forced to find a way to overcome these challenges. Luckily, my school provided me with a year-long planner, which allowed me to manage all my assignments for each class when they were due and I could add extra notes to determine how long they would take and more. I began utilizing my calendar applications on my phone and computer to assign dedicated times for studying in between practices for the many teams I am involved with. I stay organized mentally and physically too, keeping a clean and well-neated room space and back-pack, as well as having folders for every class with papers ordered by date. Online, my google drive is divided with folders for every class so I don't get lost in a sea of randomly opened documents. With these habits, I am able to go to sleep around the same time, and get around 7-8 hours of sleep which gets my mind and body ready for any hurdles I have to face the next day.
